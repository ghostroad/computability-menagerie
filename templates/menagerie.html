The coloring of classes contains a great deal of information about their properties and their relations to other classes. We recommend enabling the color key (click "Key"). The coloring changes depending on how many classes are selected and the key changes accordingly. Here are a few things to try:

  • Select/unselect classes by clicking on them.
  • Switch between coloring by "measure" (Hausdorff dimension, packing dimension and Lebesgue measure) and category using the dropdown.
  • Double-click on a class to see its properties in detail (opens a new tab). Alternatively, hover over the class to see a short summary.
  • When a single class is selected, the graph is recolored to indicate how other classes relate to the selected class.
  • When a pair of classes is selected, classes that are intermediate between them are highlighted.
  • Select a pair of classes, then click "View proofs" to see the implications between them (opens a new tab/window).
  • Select a group of classes, then click "View subgraph" to generate a subgraph consisting only of the selected classes.
  • Select a group of classes, then click "Exclude selected" to remove those classes from the graph.
  • Bookmark the page to save your settings.
